GREEN HILLS

The Green Hills of Senator Ninoy Aquino, located in the municipality of Senator Ninoy Aquino in Sultan Kudarat, Philippines, is named in honor of the late Senator Benigno “Ninoy” Aquino Jr., a national hero known for his fight for democracy. The area was once part of a vast forested highland inhabited by indigenous peoples, particularly the Manobo and B’laan tribes. Over the years, it became a settlement for Christian migrants from Luzon and the Visayas. The municipality was officially created in 1989 through Republic Act No. 6712, separating it from the town of Kalamansig. Since then, it has developed into an agricultural and eco-tourism destination while retaining its strong cultural heritage.


The Green Hills of Senator Ninoy Aquino is renowned for its breathtaking rolling landscapes covered with lush greenery, earning it the nickname “Little Batanes of Sultan Kudarat.” The area offers panoramic views of mountain ranges, cool climate, and serene surrounngs,making it a favorite spot for nature lovers and photographers. It is accessible by land from Isulan, the provincial capital, and is ideal for outdoor activities such as hiking, camping, and sightseeing. The local government continues to promote the Green Hills as a sustainable tourism destination that balances environmental preservation with community livelihood opportunities.


Green Hills is a peaceful and beautiful community surrounded by rolling hills and lush greenery. The area is known for its clean environment, fresh air, and friendly people. Most residents enjoy simple living and take pride in taking care of nature. The place offers a calm atmosphere that reflects the people’s deep respect for their land and environment.


The culture of Green Hills is rich and colorful. The locals are known for their strong sense of unity and cooperation. They celebrate traditional festivals that showcase music, dance, and art. During these celebrations, people wear native clothes, perform folk dances, and sing traditional songs that have been passed down for generations. These cultural activities remind the people of their roots and strengthen their community bonds.


Beliefs in Green Hills are often connected to nature and spirituality. Many residents believe that the land, mountains, and rivers are sacred and must be protected. They perform rituals and prayers to thank nature for providing them with food and resources. Some still follow traditional customs that ask blessings for good harvests and safety from natural disasters.


Food is one of the most important parts of life in Green Hills. The locals love cooking and sharing meals with family and friends. Their dishes are made mostly from fresh ingredients grown in their gardens or farms. They eat vegetables, root crops, fruits, and fish. During festivals, they prepare native rice cakes, stews, and grilled dishes that everyone enjoys together.


The people of Green Hills live a simple yet joyful lifestyle. Most families depend on farming, gardening, and handicrafts for their daily needs. They work together in the fields, showing cooperation and respect for each other. Even though their life is simple, they are content because they value peace, family, and hard work.


Education and tradition go hand in hand in Green Hills. The schools teach children not only academic lessons but also local culture and values. Elders share their wisdom and stories about the community’s history. This helps the younger generation understand the importance of preserving their traditions and respecting their ancestors.


Overall, Green Hills is a place where people, nature, and culture exist in harmony. The community’s beliefs, traditions, and food reflect their love for the environment and for one another. Their way of life reminds everyone that happiness can be found in simplicity, respect, and unity.
